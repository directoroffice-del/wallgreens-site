1. Product Overview

1.1 Introduction

The StiMBERTM J70 is a patented, high-performance engineered formwork runner developed by Wallgreens Formsolutions LLP, Mumbai. It combines the structural strength of a hot-dip galvanized steel rectangular tube with the nailability of a solid timber insert — creating a composite beam that is stronger, safer, more durable and more economical than conventional timber runners.

The J70 is designed as a direct drop-in replacement for wooden and aluminium H beams as well as all conventional solid timber runners — silver wood, pine wood, hollow steel channels and LVL beams. Its slotted steel tube design allows nailing of plywood shuttering directly through the slots without any proprietary accessories, making it fully compatible with existing conventional formwork systems at zero additional investment.

The J70 is available in three variants differentiated by the timber insert material. The steel tube, end caps, dimensions and fixing hardware are identical across all three variants.

Correct orientation in slab use. Place J70 beams with the slotted 50 mm face upward. Nails pass downward through the slots into the timber insert. The golden-yellow timber insert visible through the slots is a quick visual confirmation of correct orientation.

3. Pre-Use Inspection

Inspect every beam upon delivery and before each use. Notify Wallgreens immediately of any defective material received.

3.1 Steel Tube Checks

  • Galvanizing intact — no bare metal patches, rust streaks or flaking zinc
  • No visible bends, kinks or permanent deformation along length
  • Slots are clean, free of concrete blockages, burrs or sharp edges
  • End caps present and firmly attached at both ends
  • No cracking or splitting of weld seam
  • Straightness: lay beam on flat surface; maximum bow must not exceed L/500 (7.2 mm for a 3.6 m beam)

3.2 Timber Insert Checks

  • Golden yellow paint coat present and largely intact (Basic variant)
  • No visible splits, cracks or fractures along the grain
  • No bore holes, frass or tunnels indicating pest infestation
  • No fungal growth or discolouration indicating rot
  • Timber firmly seated — should not rattle or shift inside tube
  • Plus variant: OSB surface intact with no delamination or swelling
  • Pro variant: Plastic wrapping continuous with no tears

3.3 Reject-for-Service Conditions

  • Permanent bow/bend exceeding L/500
  • Cracked or split steel tube; rust penetrating through galvanizing to base metal
  • Timber split along full or partial length; loose or missing insert
  • Missing or detached end caps
  • Evidence of prior overloading — crushed sections or permanent deformation
  • Pro variant: LVL core exposed over more than 10% of beam length
Do not repair on site. Damaged J70 beams must NOT be repaired, welded, bent back or have inserts replaced on site. All repairs must be performed at Wallgreens' facility. Using damaged beams creates serious risk of formwork collapse and injury.

4. Installation & Usage — SOP

4.1 System Assembly Overview

The J70 functions as the secondary beam (runner) in a conventional prop, cup lock or ring lock supported formwork system. The assembly hierarchy from bottom to top:

LevelComponent
1 (Base)Adjustable steel props / cuplock / ringlock at designed spacing
24-way prop head or fork head on top of the staging
3Primary beams — aluminium or wooden H beams, steel channels (one direction)
4StiMBERTM J70 secondary runners (perpendicular to primary beams)
5 (Top)Plywood shuttering nailed to J70 through the slots

4.2 SOP — Slab Formwork

Step 1: Design Verification

  1. Obtain approved formwork design from structural engineer specifying prop, primary beam and J70 spacing.
  2. Confirm specified J70 span does not exceed safe working load for slab thickness and pour rate.
  3. Mark out prop positions on the floor below per approved design.

Step 2: Prop Setting

  1. Erect adjustable props at designed spacing. Check plumb using a spirit level.
  2. Tighten prop collars securely. Prop must support full dead and live load from slab.
  3. Set prop height accounting for primary beam + J70 beam + plywood thickness.

Step 3: Primary Beam Placement

  1. Place primary H beams or steel channels across prop heads in designed direction.
  2. Seat firmly in the 4-way fork head. Do not allow primary beams to cant or sit off-centre.
  3. Check primary beams are level with a straight edge and spirit level.

Step 4: J70 Beam Placement

  1. Place J70 beams perpendicular to primary beams at designed spacing (typically 200-400 mm c/c depending on slab thickness and design).
  2. Orient beams with the 70 mm dimension vertical and slots facing upward. Golden-yellow timber insert must be visible through slots from above.
  3. Beams placed end-to-end must break over a primary beam, not in mid-span.
  4. Provide minimum 100 mm end bearing on supports.

Step 5: Plywood & Nailing

  1. Lay plywood shuttering across J70 beams. Stagger joints over beam centrelines.
  2. Nail plywood down through the slots into the timber insert. Never nail through the steel tube face.
  3. Minimum 2 nails per beam-to-plywood contact at available slot positions.
  4. Check plywood alignment and surface level. Apply form release oil before concreting.

Step 6: Pre-Pour Final Checks

  1. Verify all props are plumb, tightened and at correct height.
  2. Verify all J70 beams are correctly oriented (slots up), properly seated and not overhanging excessively.
  3. Verify plywood is fully nailed, all edges supported, release oil applied.
  4. Verify no beams are cracked, bent or showing signs of damage.
  5. Obtain written sign-off from the site/formwork engineer before commencing pour.
Concrete pour rate. Concrete must be poured at the rate specified in the formwork design. Excessive speed, impact from concrete bucket drops, or vibrator contact with J70 beams can cause overloading and formwork failure.

4.3 SOP — Column Formwork

  1. Erect plywood panels to column lines against the reinforcement cage.
  2. Position J70 beams vertically against plywood back face at designed horizontal spacing. Nail plywood onto the J70.
  3. Pass tie rods through the plywood and through J70 slots — eliminating the need for separate soldier profiles.
  4. Torque tie rods uniformly before concrete placement.

4.4 SOP — Wall / Retaining Wall Formwork

  • J70 beams run horizontally across the plywood face at designed vertical intervals.
  • Slots must align with tie rod positions — plan layout before erection.
  • Diagonal raker props must anchor to a fixed point capable of resisting the full horizontal force.
  • Formwork height must not exceed the approved lateral pressure design limit.

5. Striking & Deshuttering

5.1 Minimum Striking Times

Striking must not commence until concrete has achieved sufficient strength per IS 456 and as directed by the project structural engineer. The table below is a general guide only:

Structural ElementOPC Concrete (min.)PPC / Blended Cement
Slab / Beam — full prop removal14-21 days (per design)21-28 days
Column sides24-48 hours48-72 hours
Retaining walls24-48 hours48-72 hours
Never strike early. Early striking is a leading cause of slab collapse. Always obtain written approval from the structural engineer and confirm adequate cube strength test results before striking.

5.2 Deshuttering Procedure

  1. Obtain written clearance from structural engineer confirming adequate concrete strength.
  2. Loosen prop adjustors gradually and uniformly. Do not release props suddenly or all at once.
  3. Remove tie rods from column/wall forms carefully. Do not use the J70 as a lever.
  4. Lower the formwork assembly carefully. Do not drop, throw or allow impact loading on J70 beams.
  5. Extract nails by bracing the nail puller against the plywood — never against the J70 end cap or tube.
Nail removal — critical. The most common cause of beam damage during deshuttering is levering against the J70 end cap or tube. Always brace the nail puller against the plywood sheet. Levering against the J70 bends end caps and splits the timber insert.

6. Cleaning & Maintenance

6.1 Cleaning After Each Use

  1. Remove loose concrete and mortar with a plastic scraper while material is still in the green stage.
  2. For hardened concrete use a wire brush or light tapping with a rubber mallet. Do not use steel hammers directly on the tube face.
  3. Do not use acid washes (e.g., hydrochloric acid) — they irreversibly destroy the galvanized coating.
  4. After cleaning, inspect galvanizing. Touch up minor scratches with cold galvanizing spray (minimum 95% zinc content).
  5. Basic variant: Re-apply golden yellow enamel paint (IS 5, Shade 356) to any bare or weathered timber visible through slots.
  6. Pro variant: Seal any small tears in plastic wrapping with UV-resistant waterproof tape pending return to Wallgreens for formal repair.
Note. Concrete inside slots will prevent nailing in subsequent uses. Clear all slots after every use with a screwdriver or stiff rod.

7. Storage

7.1 On-Site Storage

Ground Preparation

  • Storage area must be flat, firm and well-drained. Do not store on soft or waterlogged ground.
  • Place timber or rubber packers at ground level — minimum 2 supports per beam, spaced max 1.0 m apart, giving minimum 100 mm ground clearance.
  • Align all packers so beams lie flat without induced bow or twist.

Stacking

  • Stack beams in layers with spacer battens between each layer. Battens must be vertically aligned between layers.
  • Maximum recommended stack height: 2.0 m (~8-10 layers for 3.6 m beams).
  • Do not step on or walk over stacked beams.
  • Face all beam slots in the same direction for visual inspection and slot cleaning without unstacking.
  • Sort and stack beams by length — do not mix lengths in the same stack.

Weather Protection

  • Cover stacked beams with a breathable tarpaulin or shade net. Do not use impermeable plastic sheeting that traps moisture.
  • Basic variant: Never store in direct contact with rain or standing water — timber swelling within the tube can make the insert difficult to service.
  • In coastal or high-humidity environments, apply a thin rust-preventive spray to the steel tube.
Fire hazard. Do not store J70 beams near open flames, welding operations or flammable materials. The timber insert is combustible.

7.2 Warehouse / Depot Storage

  • Store in a dry, enclosed space away from direct sunlight and moisture.
  • Use racking systems with rubber padding on shelf contact points to prevent galvanizing damage.
  • Maintain minimum 1.2 m aisle width between stacks for safe access.
  • Clearly separate and tag damaged/quarantined beams from serviceable stock.

8. Handling & Transport

8.1 Manual Handling

  • Always carry J70 beams with the 70 mm depth held vertically. Carrying flat increases risk of permanent bow.
  • Two-person carry is recommended for all lengths of 2.4 m and above (a 3.6 m beam weighs ~16.2 kg).
  • Do not carry more than two beams per person at a time.
  • Always use cut-resistant gloves — galvanized tube edges and slot cuts can be sharp.
  • Do not drag beams across the floor or across each other — this scratches the galvanizing.
  • Never use J70 beams as levers, crowbars or stepping platforms.

8.2 Crane & Sling Handling

  1. Bundle same-length beams together — maximum 20 beams per lift for 3.6 m lengths; 30 for 1.8 m and below.
  2. Use flat-woven polyester slings (minimum 2-tonne WLL) — never chain slings or wire rope directly on beams.
  3. Place softwood timber packers between sling and beams to protect galvanizing.

8.3 Vehicle Transport

  1. Load beams on truck/trailer with timber packers at 1.0 m intervals to prevent vibration damage.
  2. Secure with ratchet straps. Do not over-tighten to the point of crushing beams.
  3. Beams must not protrude more than 1.0 m beyond the vehicle body without a red flag marker.
  4. Do not stack heavy materials on top of J70 beams during transport.
  5. Inspect beams for transport damage immediately upon every delivery.

9.2 Critical Safety Rules

Rule 1 — Never overload. Never exceed the design load on J70 beams. Concentrated loads from concrete buckets, pump lines or equipment placed on unsupported beams are a leading cause of formwork failure.
Rule 2 — No unauthorised modifications. Never cut, weld, drill or modify J70 beams. The composite section depends on the integrity of both steel tube and timber insert acting together. Any modification voids warranty and creates structural risk.
Rule 3 — No improvised connections. Only use approved formwork hardware. Improvised connections, tie wire fixings or ad-hoc clamps are prohibited.
Rule 4 — Engineer approval required. A qualified structural engineer must design and approve all formwork using J70 beams before erection. Improvising beam spacings, spans or prop arrangements on site is strictly prohibited.
Rule 5 — Inspect before every use. Every beam must pass the pre-use inspection (Section 3) before placement. A beam that fails inspection must be withdrawn from service until assessed by Wallgreens.

9.3 Emergency — Signs of Formwork Distress

If any of the following are observed during or after concrete placement, stop work immediately, evacuate the area, and report to the engineer:

  • Audible cracking sounds from the formwork system
  • Visible sagging or excessive deflection of the slab soffit
  • Cracking, bowing or failure of any J70 beam
  • Settlement or tilting of any prop
  • Concrete blowout through joints or gaps in shuttering
  • Any sudden or unexpected movement of the formwork
Evacuate first. In any sign of imminent formwork failure, evacuate all personnel from the affected zone IMMEDIATELY. Do not attempt to shore up or repair formwork while concrete is being placed or is in the green state.

10. Do's & Don'ts — Quick Reference

Do

  • Inspect beams before every use
  • Orient 70 mm depth vertically, slots upward
  • Nail only through slots — never through tube face
  • Ensure minimum 100 mm end bearing on supports
  • Place beam joints only over primary beams
  • Use cut-resistant gloves when handling
  • Clean beams thoroughly after every use
  • Clear all slots of concrete after every use
  • Store off ground with packers, covered
  • Use an engineer-approved formwork design
  • Two-person carry for beams 2.4 m and above
  • Apply release oil to plywood before every pour

Don't

  • Use damaged, bent or rusty beams
  • Cut, weld, drill or modify beams
  • Nail through the steel tube face
  • Use beams as scaffold or walkways
  • Lever against end caps during striking
  • Drag beams across floor or across each other
  • Use acid wash for cleaning
  • Place beam joints in mid-span
  • Exceed the approved concrete pour rate
  • Strike formwork before engineer clearance
  • Repair damaged beams on site
