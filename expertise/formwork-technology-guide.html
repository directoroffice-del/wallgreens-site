Formwork Systems · Technical Guide

Formwork Technology Guide for Indian Construction

A comprehensive reference for engineers and project managers on selecting, designing, and executing formwork for Indian construction projects.

What Is Formwork?

Formwork is the temporary mould into which fresh concrete is poured and retained until it achieves sufficient strength to be self-supporting. It is one of the largest cost components of concrete construction — typically representing 35–60% of the total cost of cast-in-situ concrete work.

The choice of formwork system directly impacts cycle time, concrete finish quality, material wastage, labour productivity, and overall project safety. Selecting the right system for a given project type is one of the most important decisions a construction engineer will make.

Types of Formwork Systems

1. Slab Formwork

Slab formwork supports the horizontal pour of floor slabs. The most common systems in India are:

  • Traditional timber: Sawn timber primary beams, secondary joists, and plywood decking. Low initial cost but variable quality and slow cycle times.
  • Modular systems (e.g. Mach Deck): Aluminium primary beams with pre-fabricated plywood panels. Higher initial investment, but 5–7 day cycles and consistent finish.
  • Table forms: Pre-assembled floor-by-floor forms moved intact by crane. Fastest cycle times for repetitive residential towers.

2. Wall and Column Formwork

Wall and column formwork retains concrete at fresh concrete pressure during a vertical pour. Systems include:

  • Aluminium panel systems (e.g. Mach AluPly): Lightweight, reusable, high concrete pressure capacity. Best for high-rise walls and columns.
  • Steel panel systems: High strength, long service life. Heavier than aluminium — less suitable for manual handling.
  • Timber and plywood: Flexible, low cost, poor reuse. Still widely used in India for non-standard elements.

Load Design Principles

Formwork must be designed to safely carry:

  • Dead load — self-weight of concrete (typically 24–26 kN/m³), formwork, and reinforcement
  • Live load — construction traffic, equipment, and personnel (minimum 1.5 kN/m² per IS 14687)
  • Dynamic loads — vibration during concrete placement and compaction
  • Lateral pressure — fresh concrete pressure on vertical formwork (maximum = ρgh for full fluid head)

IS 14687:1999 provides the Indian standard for design of formwork for temporary structures. All Wallgreens products are designed and certified to comply with this standard.

How to Select the Right System

The best formwork system depends on five key factors:

  1. Project type — residential tower, commercial, infrastructure, or industrial
  2. Repetition — how many identical floors/elements will use the same formwork
  3. Programme — target cycle time and overall project duration
  4. Labour availability — skilled vs. unskilled workforce on site
  5. Concrete specification — finish quality required (rough, fair-faced, or architectural)

For projects with 15+ identical floors, modular aluminium systems like Mach Deck typically deliver the lowest total formwork cost per m², despite higher upfront investment. For non-repetitive or low-rise work, traditional timber remains cost-competitive.
