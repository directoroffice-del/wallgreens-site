Choosing the Right Plywood for Formwork

Not all shuttering plywood is equal. Learn how to evaluate quality and make the right choice for your concrete finish requirements.

Why Plywood Selection Matters

The shuttering plywood is the face that contacts fresh concrete. Its surface quality, dimensional stability, and moisture resistance directly determine the concrete surface finish. Poor plywood choice leads to concrete defects, delamination mid-pour, and increased remedial plastering costs.

Key Quality Parameters

1. Film Coating Weight

The phenol film on the concrete face protects the wood from moisture and delivers a smooth, releasable surface. Film weights available in India:

Film WeightQuality LevelReuse Cycles
80 g/m²Economy / low grade5–10 cycles
120 g/m²Standard / commercial15–20 cycles
220 g/m²Premium / architectural30–50 cycles

Wallgreens Film Faced Plywood uses 120 g/m² phenol film as standard — delivering 15–20 reuse cycles for typical construction conditions.

2. Glue Bond Classification

All formwork plywood must use WBP (Weather and Boil Proof) bond classified glue. WBP glue maintains panel integrity when subjected to water absorption during concrete curing.

Reject any plywood marketed for formwork use that does not explicitly state WBP bond. Interior-grade plywood (MR bond) will delaminate when exposed to wet concrete.

3. Core Species

The core veneer species determines the panel's stiffness, weight, and moisture resistance:

  • Eucalyptus core: Dense, stiff, good moisture resistance. The most common core species for Indian formwork plywood. Good reuse performance.
  • Hardwood mixed core: Variable quality. Performance depends on species mix — inspect certificate of origin.
  • Poplar/softwood core: Lighter but less stiff. Suitable for light formwork only. Not recommended for primary deck use.

4. Thickness Selection

ThicknessApplicationMax Secondary Beam Spacing
12 mmMach Deck deck replacement, light slabs400 mm
15 mmGeneral slab formwork500 mm
18 mmHeavy slabs, wall formwork600 mm
25 mmVery heavy or wide-span applications800 mm

How to Identify Quality Plywood on Site

  • Check for IS 4990 or EN 636-3 marking on panel face
  • Inspect panel edges — core voids should be minimal and filled
  • Film should be uniform in colour with no bubbles, wrinkles, or peel
  • Panel should be flat — no bowing or cupping from storage
  • Tap panel with knuckle — solid sound indicates good core; hollow patches indicate voids
