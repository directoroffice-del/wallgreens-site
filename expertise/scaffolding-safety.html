Scaffolding Safety Best Practices

Essential safety guidelines for scaffolding design, installation, inspection, and use on Indian construction sites.

Why Scaffolding Safety Matters

Falls from height account for the majority of fatal accidents on Indian construction sites. Scaffolding failures — whether due to overloading, improper assembly, deteriorated components, or inadequate bracing — are preventable with correct planning and inspection.

This guide provides a practical framework for safe scaffolding use, based on Indian Standards IS 3696 and IS 2750, and aligned with international best practice.

Indian Standards for Scaffolding

  • IS 3696 Part 1 & 2: Safety code for scaffolds — covers design, erection, inspection, and dismantling
  • IS 2750: Specifications for steel scaffolding tubes and fittings
  • IS 1161: Specifications for ERW steel tubes used in scaffolding
  • IS 14687: Guidelines for formwork — covers temporary works safety

Pre-Erection Inspection Checklist

Before any scaffolding is erected, the following checks should be completed:

  • Ground bearing capacity confirmed — no soft spots, trenches, or voids beneath base plates
  • All tubes inspected for straightness — reject any tube with bend > 1 in 500
  • Cups, blades, and rosettes inspected — reject any with cracks, distortion, or missing welds
  • Base plates in good condition — no missing or damaged screw jacks
  • Adequate supply of cross-braces — minimum 1 brace every 3 bays
  • Safe Working Load (SWL) confirmed for intended application
  • Competent person assigned for erection supervision

Common Scaffolding Failure Modes

Failure ModeCausePrevention
Global collapseOverloading, insufficient bracing, foundation failureDesign check, bracing compliance, ground inspection
Local bucklingEccentric loading, damaged tubes, excessive jack extensionTube inspection, max 300mm jack extension
Foundation settlementSoft ground, waterlogged soilSole boards, ground investigation
Connection failureCup not hammered closed, corroded bladesPre-erection inspection, hammer check all cups
Fall of personNo guardrails, inadequate planking, no toe boardFull platform specification per IS 3696

Safe Working Load Guidelines

Never exceed the manufacturer's specified Safe Working Load for any scaffolding component:

  • Ring Lock: 60 kN per standard — do not stack loads above this per leg
  • Cup Lock: 40 kN per standard — appropriate for residential and moderate loads
  • Aluminium towers: 250 kg per platform (narrow frame), 500 kg (wide frame)
  • Apply a minimum factor of safety of 2.0 to all calculated design loads
  • Do not use scaffolding for material storage unless load is included in design
